Kazakh musician instruments

Qobyz

A bowed musical instrument with two hair strings. It was made from one solid piece of wood - juniper (arsha), maple, pine, birch.

Zhetigen

An ancient seven-string plucked instrument, shaped like a harp or a recumbent harp. The most ancient type of zhetygen was an oblong box made from a piece of wood.

Dabyl

Percussion instrument, rim with handle on both sides covered with leather. Usually the dabyl was used by warriors (on the offensive). It was an instrument with a very strong loud sound, used to give signals.

Sybyzgy

Among the wind instruments, the sybyzgy was the most beloved instrument - it became closely integrated into the musical life of the people. Sybyzgy was made from a hollow reed tube, on which three holes were cut.

Dombra

Percussion musical instrument. It was a tambourine: a rim covered on one side with leather, inside of which metal chains, rings and plates were hung, which produced an additional noise effect when struck on the skin and when moved.
